Since 1918

History of the
Chemical Corps

Over a century of defending the nation against chemical, biological, radiological, and nuclear threats. Learn the heritage that defines Dragon Soldiers.

The Legacy

A Century of
Dragon Soldiers

From the trenches of World War I to the modern battlefields of the 21st century, the Chemical Corps has stood as the nation's shield against weapons of mass destruction. This is the story of the men and women who answered the call.

1918

Chemical Warfare Service Established

On June 2, 1918, General John J. Pershing established the Chemical Warfare Service in response to Germany's devastating use of chemical weapons on the Western Front during World War I. The new service consolidated all chemical warfare activities under a single command.

1920s

Survival & Permanence

The Chemical Warfare Service was nearly eliminated in the post-war drawdown, but advocates fought to preserve the branch. Congress ultimately made it a permanent branch of the United States Army, recognizing the enduring need for chemical defense capabilities.

1942–1945

World War II

Chemical Soldiers made critical contributions across every theater — developing 4.2-inch mortar high-explosive rounds, deploying Chemical Smoke Generator Battalions to screen troop movements, employing flame weapons in the Pacific island campaigns, and establishing Camp Detrick for biological warfare research and defense.

1946

Re-designated as the Chemical Corps

The Chemical Warfare Service was officially re-designated as the "Chemical Corps" and became a recognized branch of the United States Army, reflecting its expanded mission beyond chemical warfare to include radiological defense and smoke operations.

1950–1953

Korean War

Chemical Corps Soldiers provided vital support during the Korean War through smoke operations that concealed major amphibious landings and troop movements, as well as the employment of flame and incendiary weapons in defensive and offensive operations across the peninsula.

1960s–1970s

Vietnam Era

The Chemical Corps pioneered innovative battlefield technologies in Vietnam — "people sniffer" personnel detectors, flame track vehicles and devices, herbicide operations to deny enemy concealment, and supported tunnel rat operations in the vast underground complexes of Cu Chi.

1986

Regiment & CCRA Established

The Chemical Corps Regiment was established on June 28, 1986, formalizing the regimental system for the branch. The following month in July 1986, Major General Gerald Watson founded the Chemical Corps Regimental Association to unite all Dragon Soldiers under one family.

1990–1991

Gulf War

Chemical Corps units played a central role in preparing Coalition forces to operate in the face of credible chemical and biological weapon threats from Iraq. Decontamination units, NBC reconnaissance teams, and chemical defense training proved essential to the campaign's success.

2005+

BRAC & Fort Leonard Wood

As part of the Base Realignment and Closure (BRAC) process, the Chemical Corps relocated from Fort McClellan, Alabama to Fort Leonard Wood, Missouri — consolidating the U.S. Army Chemical, Biological, Radiological, and Nuclear School alongside other maneuver support branches.

2001–2021

Global War on Terrorism

Chemical Corps Soldiers deployed continuously to Iraq and Afghanistan, conducting CBRN reconnaissance, route clearance, and hazardous material assessment missions. The 2nd Chemical Battalion and other units earned combat citations for their service in austere environments while adapting to asymmetric warfare threats.

2020

COVID-19 Response

CBRN-trained Soldiers from the Chemical Corps supported the national COVID-19 response, providing decontamination expertise, personal protective equipment training, and biological hazard assessment capabilities to military and civilian agencies during the pandemic.

Today

Defending Against 21st Century Threats

The Chemical Corps continues to evolve, integrating advanced sensors, unmanned systems, and digital detection platforms to counter chemical, biological, radiological, and nuclear threats. Dragon Soldiers remain at the forefront of protecting the force and the nation against weapons of mass destruction.
