Who We Are

About the CCRA

Founded in 1986, the Chemical Corps Regimental Association promotes the heritage, history, esprit and professionalism of the United States Army Chemical Corps.

Our Origin

Founded to Serve
Dragon Soldiers

The Chemical Corps Regimental Association was founded in July 1986 by Major General Gerald Watson, then Chief of the Chemical Corps and first Colonel of the Regiment.

The CCRA replaced both the Chemical Corps Association and the Chemical Corps Museum Foundation, unifying the Corps community under a single organization dedicated to preserving its legacy and supporting its members.

☢
MG Gerald Watson
Founder & First Colonel of the Regiment
"The CCRA was established to unite all Chemical Corps Soldiers — past, present, and future — under one regimental family, ensuring our heritage and traditions endure."

Governance

Led by Those
Who Have Served

The CCRA is managed by a Board of Directors composed of active duty, retired, and veteran officers and NCOs who volunteer their time and expertise to serve the Chemical Corps community.

⚙

Board of Directors

Composed of active duty, retired, and veteran officers and NCOs who volunteer to guide the Association's mission and programs.

📋

Biannual Review

The Board meets twice a year to review and approve plans, programs, and strategic initiatives that support Dragon Soldiers and their families.

🤝

Private Support

The Association relies entirely on private contributions and the dedication of volunteers to fund its mission — no government funding.

Our Mission

Four Pillars of
the Chemical Corps

01

Heritage

Preserving and promoting the rich history of the Chemical Corps, from its establishment in 1918 to the modern-day Dragon Soldiers who carry its legacy forward.

02

Esprit de Corps

Fostering unity, pride, and camaraderie among all Chemical Corps Soldiers — active duty, reserve, guard, retired, and veterans — as one regimental family.

03

Image

Enhancing the public understanding and recognition of the Chemical Corps' vital role in defending the nation against chemical, biological, radiological, and nuclear threats.

04

Professionalism

Advancing the technical and leadership competencies of Chemical Corps professionals through education, training support, and scholarship programs.
