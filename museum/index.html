Our Mission

Preserving the Legacy

The US Army Chemical Corps Museum honors the past, present, and future of the Chemical Regiment — the men and women who have defended the nation against chemical, biological, radiological, and nuclear threats for over a century.

Located within the John B. Mahaffey Museum Complex at Fort Leonard Wood, Missouri, the museum is open to the public and free of charge.

The Complex

John B. Mahaffey
Museum Complex

The Chemical Corps Museum is one of several military heritage museums housed within the John B. Mahaffey Museum Complex at Fort Leonard Wood — a facility dedicated to preserving the history of the Army's Engineer, Military Police, and Chemical Corps branches.

Open to the general public, the complex provides a unique opportunity to explore the history of these critical Army branches in one location, free of charge.
