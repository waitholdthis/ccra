Who We Are

Mission & Vision

Mission

"To preserve and promote the history and traditions of the U.S. Army Chemical Corps; while supporting the Soldiers and Families of the Chapter by maintaining the Chapter Legacy, Enabling Mentorship, Networking, Professional Development, and Providing Community Outreach to the Fort Bragg Community."

Vision

"To be the premier private organization on Fort Bragg which is fully aligned with the U.S. Army's Chemical Corps Regimental Association and recognized for supporting Fort Bragg Soldiers and Families."

Chapter Charter

Aims & Purposes

01

Establish a permanent organization that will further develop the common bond existing between all Dragon Soldiers who served on Fort Bragg.

02

Foster appreciation for and study of the history and traditions of the United States Army Chemical Corps and understanding of their historical roles and contribution to the development and defense of the nation.

03

Maintain records and publish the history and achievements of Fort Bragg CBRN Soldiers.

04

Maintain contact with all partners who have become disabled in the service of their Country and provide educational opportunities to their families.

05

Provide assistance and service when possible in matters pertaining to Veterans benefits.

06

Encourage the Chemical, Biological, Nuclear, and Radiological (CBRN) aspect of national defense.

07

Provide administrative and financial support to Dragon Soldiers for educational and scientific purposes, including an annual contribution to the Education Fund.

08

Provide support mechanisms for our Dragon Soldiers and their Families on Fort Bragg.

09

Recognize individuals and organizations that have contributed to the skill and professionalism of the Chemical Corps.

10

Conduct meetings, outings, and other activities to promote, educate, and organize other means of support to the Chapter.

11

Engage in or sponsor historical research and cooperate with accredited educational institutions, veterans chapters, and other organizations in furtherance of its objectives.

12

Support other organizations, private or public, which have similar objectives.
