Chemical Corps Regimental Association

Order of the Dragon

Program Overview

Honoring the Dragon Soldier

The Order of the Dragon comprises four Awards recognizing individuals who demonstrate the highest standards of integrity and moral character and professional competence in their service to the Chemical Corps.

Recipients include Dragon Soldiers — active and retired military members — as well as civilians and individuals who have made lasting contributions to advancing the Chemical Corps throughout their careers.

Recognition Levels

The Four Awards

Highest Honor

Ancient Order

The highest level of recognition within the Order of the Dragon. The Ancient Order is reserved for individuals whose distinguished service has made a profound and enduring impact on the Chemical Corps and the nation it defends. This honor represents the pinnacle of achievement within the Order.

Second Tier

Honorable Order

Recognizes exceptional and honorable contributions to the Chemical Corps mission and community. Recipients of the Honorable Order have demonstrated sustained dedication to the values and mission of the Chemical Regiment over the course of their careers.

Legionnaire Award

Honors those who have made significant and sustained contributions to the Chemical Corps. The Legionnaire Award recognizes individuals who have consistently gone above and beyond in their service, embodying the standards and traditions of the Dragon Soldier.

Carol Ann Watson Spouse Award

Recognizes the extraordinary support and contributions of Chemical Corps spouses. Named in honor of Carol Ann Watson, this award acknowledges the essential role that spouses play in sustaining the strength, resilience, and cohesion of the Chemical Regiment family.

Who Can Be Recognized

Eligibility

The Order of the Dragon is open to Dragon Soldiers and civilians alike who have made meaningful contributions to the Chemical Corps and the CCRA. Recipients may include:

  • Active duty, reserve, and retired military members who have served in the Chemical Corps
  • Civilians who have made significant contributions to advancing the mission and legacy of the Chemical Corps
  • Spouses of Chemical Corps members recognized through the Carol Ann Watson Award
  • Individuals approved by exception by the Board of Directors for conspicuous service to the Chemical Corps and the CCRA when standard criteria are not met

Special Provisions

The Board of Directors may approve an award by exception for conspicuous service to the Chemical Corps and the CCRA in cases where standard eligibility criteria are not otherwise met. Nomination criteria were last updated on October 8, 2019.

Award Protocol

Wearing Guidelines

Military Personnel

The medallion is worn around the neck with the ribbon inside the coat collar on Army Blue, White, or Green uniforms.

On mess uniforms, the medallion hangs at full ribbon length.

Civilians

Males wear the medallion at full ribbon length around the neck inside the coat collar.

Females may wear the Carol Ann Watson Award as a necklace or broach, styled appropriately for their attire.

Important: The dragon medallion must face outward and remain visible at all official Army, Service, or Department of Defense events.

Nominate a Dragon Soldier

Submit a Nomination
