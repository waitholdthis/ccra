Recognition & Support

Programs & Scholarships

The CCRA honors exceptional service members, recognizes outstanding units, and invests in the future through scholarships and awards.

Chemical Corps Hall of Fame

The Chemical Corps Hall of Fame honors CBRN personnel who have served the Chemical Corps and the Nation with exceptional distinction. This is the highest recognition bestowed by the Chemical Corps Regimental Association.

Nominees must have been retired for a minimum of two years prior to nomination. Nominations are reviewed by a selection committee and inductees are honored at the annual CCRA ceremony.

Distinguished Members of the Corps

The Distinguished Members of the Corps program recognizes individuals who have demonstrated continued, exceptional service to the Chemical Corps throughout their careers. This honor acknowledges sustained contributions that have had a lasting impact on the regiment.

The nomination process follows the same procedures as the Hall of Fame. Candidates are evaluated based on their cumulative record of service, leadership, and contributions to the Chemical Corps mission.

Order of the Dragon

The Order of the Dragon recognizes outstanding contributions to the Chemical Corps at various levels of achievement. There are four distinct awards within the Order:

Ancient Order

The highest level of recognition within the Order of the Dragon, reserved for the most distinguished service.

Honorable Order

Recognizes exceptional and honorable contributions to the Chemical Corps mission and community.

Legionnaire

Honors those who have made significant and sustained contributions to the Chemical Corps.

Carol Ann Watson Spouse Award

Recognizes the extraordinary support and contributions of Chemical Corps spouses.

MG William L. Sibert Award

Named in honor of Major General William L. Sibert, the first Chief of the Chemical Warfare Service, this prestigious award recognizes the best CBRN/CBRNE Company in the United States Army.

Companies are evaluated on their overall readiness, training proficiency, leadership, and mission execution. The Sibert Award represents the highest standard of excellence for CBRN units and serves as a benchmark for company-level performance across the force.

CSM George Murray Leadership Award

The CSM George Murray Leadership Award is a rigorous competition that tests the technical and tactical skills of Chemical Corps NCOs and Soldiers.

Competitors are evaluated on their mastery of CBRN fundamentals, physical fitness, leadership ability, and tactical proficiency. The competition fosters excellence and readiness among the enlisted ranks of the Chemical Corps, ensuring the highest standards of professional competence.

SGM Paul Cockman Scholarship Program

The SGM Paul Cockman Scholarship Program provides financial assistance to the spouses and children of CCRA members who are pursuing education at accredited post-secondary schools.

This scholarship honors the legacy of SGM Paul Cockman and reflects the CCRA's commitment to supporting Chemical Corps families. Applicants must be dependents of current CCRA members in good standing and enrolled or accepted at an accredited college, university, or vocational institution.
